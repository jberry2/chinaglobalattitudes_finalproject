PEW RESEARCH CENTER - Global Attitudes Project

Spring 2011 23 Nation Survey

It is important to read this information before using the 2011 Pew Global Attitudes Survey dataset. 

 
1.	General population weight:
	
	The weight that must be used when analyzing the attitudes and opinions of the general populations are:
		
	General population variable name:  Weight
			
	Weight by weight for this analysis. 


2.	In Pakistan, two surveys were fielded in Spring 2011, one before the raid that killed Osama bin Laden (April 10-26, 2011) and one after the raid (May 8-15, 2011)

	For the April Pakistan survey, country=16. Country label is "Pakistan".
 	
	For the May Pakistan survey, country=24. Country label is "Pakistan refield".

	Weight variable is the same for both surveys (weight by weight for analysis) 


3.	In Japan, these was a recontact survey to ask about the effects of the March 2011 tsunami on individual lives.

	The variables in this recontact survey were RE_JAP6a, RE_JAP6b, RE_JAP6c, RE_JAP6d, RE_JAP6e, and RE_JAP6g.

	Weight by weight for this analysis. 
		

4.	The full topline results are for the general populations, unless otherwise stated. To access topline trends, please use the Pew Global Attitudes Project Question Database on our website: http://www.pewglobal.org/question-search/     


5.	The questionnaire includes full question wording as well as answer category values.


6.	The Methods in Detail provides methodological overviews of each survey country, including sample design, mode, languages, fieldwork dates, sample size, margin of error, and representativeness.  


7.	Certain variables have been eliminated from the dataset either because of disclosure sensitivities or because of administrative errors.
